Based in Wexford, Tyrrell Coachworks provides metal fabrication for the Irish, UK and European market using a selection of materials including steel, aluminium, brass and copper.

The History of Metal Shaping

Coachwork is a term used to describe the body of a horse drawn carriage, and when the horse was replaced by the internal combustion engine coach builders quickly adapted their skills to what would be the greatest innovation in transport since the invention of the wheel. Whilst the bodywork of vehicles has evolved over the last 120 years, the art of metal shaping has remained virtually unchanged.
Tyrrell Coachworks has a family history dating back to the 1920s when our grandfather Tom O Brien, worked in the earliest bodyshops in Dublin.
